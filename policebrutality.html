What is Police Brutality?

Police Brutality is a form of police Misconduct, when police use excessive force. This is generally towards people of color. Examples of police Misconduct include

Race Profiling - Making assumations based on someone's race as a reason to accuse them of a crime

Witness Tampering - If a police officer(s) try to change a witness testimony or take action to make sure they are prevented from testifiying they have commit police misconduct

Briding Lawmakers- Police officer(s) sometimes brib lawmakers in order for them to keep or pass laws that give them the most power

Illegal Search and Seizures & False Arrest - This is when the police illegally search an area without having the right to do so. If they believe they have found something it could lead to a false arrest becuase they had no right to search the area in the first place.

This is only a few examples of Police Misconduct, there are many more. If you are still unsure of what police misconduct is you can go to Halt.org to know some real-life examples of police brutality.

What to do during Police Encounters

Getting stopped by the police can be a nerve recking experience becuase you never know what type of police you will encounter or how a routine traffic stop will end. Here's a list of action you should consider doing when by stopped by the police.

Be Respectful and Speak when Spoken to

Do not make the situation worst by being hostile towards the officer. Hostility will not solve the situation. Be respectful and speak when spoken and remain calm during the sitation.

Know Your Rights

You do not have to talk to the police nor allow them to search your vehicle if you feel uncomfortable. If you are under arrest make sure you are read your Miranda Rights. If you are unsure of what they are. Visit MirandaRights.org

Keep Your Hands Visible

keep your hands on the steering wheel so that the officers have no reason to suspect anything. Before you reach for anything driver's license, insurence etc. make sure the officer(s) is fully aware of what you are doing. Also make sure the interior of your car is well lit to allow the officer(s) to see everything.

Unsure of the Police officer is a real officer?

If you are unsure that the police officer is a real officer, ask for some identification or a badge number. But most of all if you feel like you are a victim of police brutality record the sitation with your phone and make sure you have evidence of the event. Also soon after file a police misconduct report.
